City Council Meeting

10/06/2025

This is what all I took notes on for the city meeting

The first roughly 28 minutes they were taking pictures and stuff.

The Council voted to approve a bunch of money stuff. The big things were voting yes on a new plan to issue $17.7 million in city bonds and setting aside $20 million to build a facility at the wastewater plant that will make fertilizer, which is a huge project. They also shuffled some cash around for things like storm drain work and to pay for forensic nursing services.

They also made September 21st Opioid Awareness Day. They read a paper that talked about how bad the fentanyl crisis is and how the city needs to help stop people from dying. They noted that deaths from fentanyl are rising really fast and they gave a shoutout to the Tulsa Fire Department's team that goes out to help people with overdoses, gives out Narcan, and tries to get people into recovery.

Finally, they said yes to a few building/land things. They approved the final plan for the Crossover Community Development and okayed a couple of requests to change zoning rules. Plus, they got rid of a $300 fee for a guy whose yard got messed up after the big 2023 Father's Day storm. The city admitted they messed up the cleanup paperwork process, so they wiped the fee. They also approved a plan to fix up a dinosaur statue at Howard Park to help with Route 66 tourism.
